Committee Roles

Chair

The chair’s responsibilities include overseeing vision casting for the fellowship. The chair is tasked with understanding the trajectory HCCF is heading towards and leading the committee accordingly. The goal of the chair is to also take care of the committee to make sure they are able to take care of their own leaders, and subsequently the rest of the fellowship. The chair is consistently praying and listening to God’s voice and direction for the fellowship.

Vice Chair

The vice chair acts as the “silent watcher.” While the chair sets up the vision/theme/trajectory for the year, the vice-chair is watching the response of the fellowship to it. The vice-chair also helps with the chair’s thought process. If you have a concern or suggestion about the fellowship or committee that doesn’t concern a specific ministry, the vice-chair is the point person. They as well take care of the administrative side of things from booking rooms, contacting MSU, and maintaining/sending out the emails/facebook announcements.

Men’s/Women’s Ministry

The head of Men’s/Women’s Ministry is responsible of overseeing the gender specific Discipleship Groups (DG). As well, fostering a community that encourages Godly manhood/womanhood. The head of men’s and women’s ministry often collaborate together. Practically, the discipleship group leaders are trained by the men’s and women’s ministry leader. Which may include the selection of DGL, training them, and holding regular meetings. They are as well responsible for caring for the DGL’s as they carry out their ministry throughout the year.

Worship Ministry

The head of Worship Ministry is responsible of taking care of musical worship in the fellowship through facilitating unity and joy in God through corporate praise before Him. Practical tasks include enlisting team leaders and worship team members, forming teams, creating worship schedules, and helping to determine praise and prayer formats. They as well take care of the spiritual and mental health of worship leaders through meeting with them consistently.

Year Ministry

The head of Years Ministry helps guide the year leaders as they lead their own individual years. He/she keeps track of the needs of each years, while praying continually for them, providing guidance for struggles and executes the inter-year prayer ministry. Practical tasks include creating a space/avenue for inter year discipleship, and meeting with year leaders to pray to intercede on a. year’s behalf. They as well organize inter-year prayer meeting(s) by lading or recruiting help and guide. The head of the Year ministry acts as the communication link between committee and each year, especially for year involvement during retreat, Friday night programs, and Grad Banquet responsibility delegations.

External Ministries

The head of External Ministries is responsible for helping to support and start-up various ministries focused on external outreach. This role will be in charge of the leaders of Welcome Team, Intramurals, Retreat Planning Group, Seekers, and various inter fellowship activities. With intramurals they are responsible of finding captains, and helping to coordinate team sign ups. Within the welcome team ministry they help recruit members/leaders. For RPG they are in charge of starting up RPG meetings before passing on this responsibility to RPG leaders. For the seekers ministry the head of external ministries is responsible of taking care of the leaders, and integrating the ministry into the fellowship. Lastly, the interfellowship ministry is where HCCF collaborates with different McMaster fellowships. The external ministries leader will be responsible for acting as the representative for HCC, and relaying information back to committee.
